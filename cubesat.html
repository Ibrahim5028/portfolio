ESSENCE CubeSat Project

About ESSENCE

The ESSENCE project at York University is centered around the launch of a 3U CubeSat, a small satellite comparable in size to three stacked Rubik's Cubes. This satellite, equipped with a wide-angle camera from Canadensys, is dedicated to monitoring the northern permafrost. Launched aboard a Space X Falcon 9 rocket in July 2023 it was deployed from the International Space Station via NanoRacks.

About the Project


In the ESSENCE project, my key role involved testing control boards for grounding and continuity, crucial due to their static charge sensitivity. I ensured thorough functionality checks of all components. Additionally, I led the design and manufacture of control board racks, demanding high precision.


This task required the use of CAD (Computer-Aided Design) for detailed planning and CNC machining to achieve tight tolerances of +/- 0.05 inches, applying Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) for accuracy. Furthermore, I effectively communicated our progress, presenting to the Canadian Space Agency and York University, ensuring alignment with our launch schedule.


Skill Acquired:


Electrical Testing, Component Inspection, Precision Design and Manufacturing, CAD/CNC Proficiency
