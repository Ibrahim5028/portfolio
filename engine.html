Engine Project

About the Project


As the leader of the Fuel and Air Division in the Lassonde Motorsports club, I managed a team of 20 students to enhance an engine's efficiency, reliability, and performance. We improved the compression ratio using domed pistons, upgraded to an electronic ignition system for better combustion efficiency, and focused heavily on improving airflow through enhanced intake and exhaust systems. The engine we worked on was a Suzuki 4 Cylinder 800cc F8a Carburetor Engine.


We installed a turbocharger/supercharger for increased air intake and power and rebuilt worn components using CAD and machining tools. Additionally, we utilized ECU tuning for optimized fuel-air mixture and ignition timing, which significantly modernized the engine. In terms of performance, we found that the engine after all the modifications had a 12% reduction in fuel usage while having its power increase from 53 hp to 75 hp.


Skill Acquired:


Team Management, Mechanical Modification, Airflow Optimization, CAD and Machining, ECU Tuning, Performance Analysis
