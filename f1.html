F1 Car Project

About the Project


I am currently leading my final year project group and our main goal is to design, build, and refine the chassis and body of a Formula 1 race car. We are trying to blend advanced engineering concepts to practical real-world applications.


Focusing on the chassis and bodywork, our aim in the Formula SAE competition is to demonstrate our engineering expertise by constructing a race car that excels in performance, innovation, and efficiency. The chassis and body are to be designed and optimized with strength and weight reduction in mind.


We have successfully completed the design phase for both the chassis and body of our Formula car, laying the groundwork for the manufacturing stage next semester. Throughout the design process, we extensively utilized CAD software for precision modeling and Finite Element Method (FEA) simulations to predict the stress and performance of our designs under real-world conditions.


As we transition into the manufacturing phase, we're preparing to use tools like milling machines, welding equipment, and testing machines to transform our digital designs into physical reality. This hands-on approach will allow us to apply the insights gained from our CAD and FEA simulations, ensuring the car is built to withstand the rigors of racing.


Skill Acquired:


Team Leadership and Project Management, Advanced Engineering Design, CAD and FEA Proficiency, Manufacturing Skills, Adherence to Standards
