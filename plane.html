RC Plane Project

About the Project


I took on the challenge of designing a custom-built, remote-controlled plane, drawing on fluid dynamics and aircraft control systems to craft a model that balanced agility and stability.


I initiated the project with a comprehensive Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) to systematically pinpoint and address potential failure points and their root causes, ensuring the design's resilience.


Utilizing CATIA, I meticulously crafted the plane's structure, paying close attention to dimension tolerances crucial for achieving optimal aerodynamic control and efficient lift. This precision was vital for the aircraft's overall performance.


To assess durability, I conducted Finite Element Analysis (FEA) within CATIA, evaluating the foam body's resistance to impacts from various heights. This was key to ensuring the prototype could withstand crashes without significant damage.


The university's wind tunnel was instrumental in testing the prototype, allowing me to measure lift, drag, and stability. These tests were crucial for fine-tuning the plane's aerodynamics, leading to significant performance improvements.


Lastly, I applied Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) analysis using Star-CCM+ to refine the wing design. By smoothing out sharp edges, I enhanced lift, reduced drag, and extended the plane's flight time, culminating in a design that epitomized efficiency and resilience.


Skill Acquired:


Aerodynamics Understanding, Mechanical Design, Hands-On Assembly, Control Systems Insight, Problem-Solving and Innovation
