Rocket Engine Project

About the Project


As a mechanical engineering student passionate about rocketry, I'm designing a rocket engine using CAD software. This project is enhancing my understanding of rocket engine mechanics, including propulsion, fluid dynamics, and thermodynamics, and allowing me to apply theoretical knowledge to practical design.


A key part of my design process is using Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) to ensure precision and manufacturability of the engine components. This approach is crucial for the components' functionality and reliability under extreme conditions.


I conducted a thermal simulation on the rocket engine components to evaluate the temperature distribution at peak operating conditions, specifically when the combustion chamber reaches 2773 K. This analysis aimed to identify hotspots and assess potential enhancements for more effective heat dissipation.


Through this project, I'm not only developing skills in precision engineering and problem-solving but also advancing towards my goal of building a rocket and exploring the fundamentals of rocketry.


Skill Acquired:


CAD Proficiency, Understanding of Rocket Mechanics, Application of Theoretical Knowledge, Precision Engineering, Manufacturability Focus, Thermal Analysis
